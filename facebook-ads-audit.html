Two bigger findings
Finding 01 · Creative volume

One winning ad isn't a strategy — a testing engine is

Each rebuild above is a single direction. At your scale that's not how winning creative is found. A serious paid-social operation runs 20–30+ creative and copy variants per product — different hooks, formats, angles, proof styles — and lets spend flow to whatever the data rewards. The four ads in the library suggest creative is being made one at a time, not tested in volume.

The opportunity isn't "a better ad". It's a repeatable system: a creative matrix per hero product, structured testing, and weekly iteration on what actually converts — so every euro of media spend compounds instead of resetting.

Finding 02 · Brand vs performance

Every ad is bottom-funnel. For your size, that's expensive

All four live ads are pure performance: product, price-adjacent, "Shop Now". There is no brand layer — nothing building 8wines as a name, a point of view, or a category ("where Europe finds rare wine"). Running only performance means rising CAC, dependence on retargeting, and no pricing power to defend the "premium" positioning the site claims.

At your revenue, a brand layer isn't a luxury — it's margin defense. A modest, consistent brand-building track (story, curation, the "rare finds" identity) feeds cheaper, higher-converting performance underneath it. The two work as a system, not a choice.

The pattern underneath

Five fixes that apply to the whole account

The individual rebuilds trace back to the same five root causes. Fix these systematically and every future ad starts ahead.

01

Bottles on white don't stop the scroll

One bottle on a white background blends into the feed. Either a line-up or an atmospheric frame — the white packshot belongs on the product page, not the ad.

02

The strongest proof is buried in body copy

4.89★, 5,850 reviews, Gold 2025, "we pay all duties", 1–3 day shipping — none of it is on any creative. Proof belongs on the image, where it's seen.

03

They sell the mainstream, not the moat

Champagne and Bordeaux are in every wine shop. Georgia, Moldova, "largest Washington selection in Europe" — that's the ownable edge. Only one ad uses it.

04

Copy and image contradict each other

"Bestsellers from six regions" over a single Spanish bottle creates friction. What the eye sees must match what the text promises.

05

No price, no urgency, no reason now

Not one ad gives a reason to click today. No price anchor, no bundle saving, no time-bound offer. Conversion needs a "why now".
